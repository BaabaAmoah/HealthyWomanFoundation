Unlocking the mystery of Endometriosis

•Endometriosis is a disorder in which cells similar to the lining of the uterus, or endometrium, grow outside the uterus. It often involves the pelvic tissue and can envelop the ovaries and fallopian tubes.

  • •The main causes of Endometriosis are problems with menstrual period flow. Retrograde menstrual flow is the most likely cause of endometriosis. •Moreover, it can also be caused by genetic factors.
    It may be inherited in the genes because it runs in families. •Immune system problems are another cause.

  • •Diagnosis involves lab tests or imaging which is often required. This usually involves tests such as the pelvic exam. During a pelvic exam, your doctor manually feels (palpates) areas in your pelvis for abnormalities,such as cysts on your reproductive organs or scars behind your uterus.

  • •The most common symptoms are pain and menstrual irregularities. Endometriosis pain typically presents itself as painful menstrual cramps that may go into the abdomen (stomach) or lower back. It can also occur as pain during or after sex.

  • •The condition is treatable by a medical professional. Treatments include hormone medication and excision surgery or naturally through eating mega-3 fatty acids & fish oil supplements. •Treatments can radically reduce symptoms for many patients. Common medications include non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) like ibuprofen, or birth control pills that control hormonal fluctuations in the body.

  • •Endometriosis can affect women across all ethnic backgrounds and at any age, but it most commonly affects women during their reproductive years between the ages of 25 and 35. •The risk of developing it can be reduced by avoiding foods and chemicals that increase estrogen levels such as caffeine and alcohol. •Exercise more than four hours a week can also help prevent it.
